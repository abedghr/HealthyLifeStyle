Cumulative Sugar




What is a cumulative sugar test?
Where hemoglobin is present in red blood cells with glucose.

    Things you should know about the Cumulative Diabetes Screening
  1. Experts recommend that people with diabetes have a cumulative sugar test at least twice a year, and may be more than that, to meet your treatment goals.
  2. Cumulative glucose measurement is not always accurate, for example: if a person has a certain type of hemoglobin mutation - a difference in the structure of hemoglobin - that leads to an inaccuracy in the test.
  3. Your results may differ due to natural changes in your blood glucose level, for example: the level of blood glucose changes when eating or exercising, or illness and stress may also affect the results.
  4. Some people may get wrong results if they have kidney failure, liver disease, or severe anemia.
  5. If you get a high accumulated sugar test result, this increases your risk of cardiovascular disease and complications from diabetes.
